Our story

About Us

A non-profit, non-religious organisation registered under the Charitable Trust Act (No. 470/IV/11), depending entirely on the generosity of individuals and businesses — we receive no government funding.

How Nizhal Began

Through the pain and grief of destitute children, our founders' eyes were opened to the suffering of thousands of young children around the world — children suffering for no fault of their own, except having been born in the wrong place. We started Nizhal Charitable Trust in 2011, with a motive to help orphaned and destitute children, and the help of a few committed sponsors.

We began with 15 children, both boys and girls, cared for with our own resources. We believe children need to live in a homely environment, full of love and care, to help them grow into adulthood well-adjusted and secure.

A Home Just for Girls

From 20 May 2013, in line with government norms, our home became exclusively for girls — aged 6 to 16, all of them school-going children. Today, 13 girls call Nizhal home.

Why We Focus on Girls

The numbers are part of why this focus matters to us:

Nearly 60% of the more than 130 million primary-school-age children not enrolled in school, worldwide, are girls.
By age 18, girls have received an average of 4.4 years less education than boys.
At least one in three girls and women worldwide has been beaten or sexually abused in her lifetime.

In societies where a male child is regarded as more valuable, girls are often denied the right to life, to a name and nationality, to education, to free association, and to liberty itself — married off early, or kept home for domestic work. Nizhal Charitable Trust exists to improve the welfare of the girl child, in shelter, health, nutrition and education.

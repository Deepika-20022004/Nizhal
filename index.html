What guides us

Vision & Mission

Vision

To provide survival and development care for the forgotten children of the world.

Mission

To provide destitute and abused children with shelter, clothing, education and meals — and to create an environment that is safe, nurturing, fun and creative, where children can freely express themselves, heal, be acknowledged and heard.

We endeavour to support and empower every child in our care to explore the essence of who they are.

Our story

About Us

A non-profit, non-religious organisation registered under the Charitable Trust Act (No. 470/IV/11), depending entirely on the generosity of individuals and businesses — we receive no government funding.

How Nizhal Began

Through the pain and grief of destitute children, our founders' eyes were opened to the suffering of thousands of young children around the world — children suffering for no fault of their own, except having been born in the wrong place. We started Nizhal Charitable Trust in 2011, with a motive to help orphaned and destitute children, and the help of a few committed sponsors.

We began with 15 children, both boys and girls, cared for with our own resources. We believe children need to live in a homely environment, full of love and care, to help them grow into adulthood well-adjusted and secure.

A Home Just for Girls

From 20 May 2013, in line with government norms, our home became exclusively for girls — aged 6 to 16, all of them school-going children. Today, 13 girls call Nizhal home.

Why We Focus on Girls

The numbers are part of why this focus matters to us:

Nearly 60% of the more than 130 million primary-school-age children not enrolled in school, worldwide, are girls.
By age 18, girls have received an average of 4.4 years less education than boys.
At least one in three girls and women worldwide has been beaten or sexually abused in her lifetime.

In societies where a male child is regarded as more valuable, girls are often denied the right to life, to a name and nationality, to education, to free association, and to liberty itself — married off early, or kept home for domestic work. Nizhal Charitable Trust exists to improve the welfare of the girl child, in shelter, health, nutrition and education.

In his own words

Founder’s Message

First of all, my hearty thanks for visiting our website. My wife and I started this organisation to help and serve destitute and abused children — the “forgotten” members of our society.

I run a legal and family-welfare consultancy, where every day I handle cases — many of them divorces — and counsel children who have become depressed and insecure after the sudden loss of a parent, whether to an accident or other means.

“These children have lost so much, yet their hearts are filled with love and hope for the future. Together, we can return that love — by giving hope, education, love and protection.”

This is what moved us to open Nizhal Home — to shelter and serve children facing the harsh realities of poverty, to ease their hardship, and to help them live happy, peaceful lives. I ask everyone reading this to join us in that effort.

— R. Rajagopal, Founder & Trustee

Day to day

Facilities Provided

The doors of Nizhal are always open to orphaned and destitute children. Here is what we provide.

  • Lodging & boarding

    School uniforms, stationery, bedding and every other daily amenity a child needs.

  • Schooling & tutoring

    All children attend nearby schools, with a qualified tutor appointed for remedial study.

  • Nutrition

    A wholesome, nutritious diet to support each child's physical and mental health.

  • Sport & exercise

    Daily exercise, sport and games as part of every child's routine.

  • Individual care

    Our staff give individual attention to each child's emotional needs, working as a team that understands our goals for the children.

The children form deep bonds with one another quickly — our home carries a genuine “family” feeling, built on love and affection.
